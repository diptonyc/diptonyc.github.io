The Northern Lights

THE OTHERWORLDLY AURORA borealis,or northern lights, begin high in the Earth’s atmosphere when charged particles from the sun collide with atoms and molecules in the Earth’s magnetosphere. These energetic interactions typically occur near the magnetic poles, where the Earth's magnetic field is strongest. The result? A colorful, dancing light show that can paint the sky with glowing hues of green, pink, red, purple, and blue, depending on the type of gas involved and the altitude of the collisions. Want a fun fact? The most common color, green, comes from oxygen molecules about 60 miles above the Earth. Red auroras come from higher-altitude oxygen, and purples or blues are due to nitrogen.
